## Summary

Already triaged by a maintainer (carries `help wanted`, `chromium`, `web`, `android`, `trackpad/scroll` labels). The issue is an upstream Chromium/Android platform behavior where pointer events interrupt trackpad inertial scrolling. Keeping open as-is.

## Change summary

Scrolling momentum from a physical trackpad on Android is interrupted when fingers leave the pad, likely due to how pointer/focus events are handled in Chromium on Android.

## Best solution

This likely requires either a Chromium-side fix for how trackpad inertial scroll events are emitted on Android, or a VS Code workaround in the pointer/gesture handling (src/vs/editor/browser/controller/pointerHandler.ts or the gesture module) to avoid stealing focus or cancelling scroll momentum on pointerup. The `help wanted` label is appropriate — a community contributor familiar with Android trackpad event semantics could investigate.
